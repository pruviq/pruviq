# Legal Research: Crypto Platform Legal Pages Analysis

**Research Date:** 2026-02-16
**Research Agent:** JEPO Research Agent
**Purpose:** Analyze legal structures of major crypto platforms for PRUVIQ legal framework

---

## Executive Summary

This research analyzes the legal documentation structures of three major crypto/financial platforms (CoinGecko, TradingView, 3Commas) to identify best practices for PRUVIQ's legal framework. The analysis covers Privacy Policies, Terms of Service, financial disclaimers, GDPR/CCPA compliance, and crypto-specific risk warnings.

**Key Finding:** All platforms emphasize clear "not financial advice" disclaimers, "AS IS" service provisions, comprehensive liability limitations, and strict compliance with GDPR/CCPA requirements.

## 5. GDPR/CCPA Compliance Framework

### Cookie Consent Requirements (2026 Standards)

#### Legal Basis Mapping
- **Consent Required:** Marketing and analytics cookies
- **Legitimate Interest:** Certain analytics (with balancing tests)
- **Strictly Necessary:** Technical functionality cookies

**Critical Rule:** "Legitimate interest cannot justify non-essential cookies like analytics or marketing tracking—these require explicit user consent under both GDPR and ePrivacy Directive requirements."

#### Minimum Disclosure Requirements
1. Purpose of each cookie category (specific descriptions)
2. Duration of storage
3. Data controller identity and contact information
4. Data processors
5. Recipients of data
6. User rights including withdrawal procedures

### 2026 Enforcement Trends
> "Cookie compliance is entering a new phase of regulatory scrutiny and global alignment, with supervisory authorities in the UK, EU, United States, India, and Latin America tightening expectations around consent, user choice, and transparency, while intensifying enforcement against non-compliant practices."

**Technical Compliance:**
> "Regulators are digging into the technical side, examining cookie behavior and script loading. Any site setting analytics or marketing cookies before consent faces immediate action, which also extends to third-party scripts and pixel tracking."

### Consent Logs (Evidence)
> "Consent logs—detailed, exportable records showing who gave consent, when, and for which purposes—serve as evidence if an auditor or DPA ever asks for proof."

## 6. Affiliate Disclosure Best Practices

### FTC Requirements
> "The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) requires by law to disclose relationships that could influence the endorsement or promotion of products, and all affiliate marketers are required to do this regardless of the promotion size."

### Placement & Language
> "If there's any material connection between you and a product or merchant, disclose it in plain, unavoidable language, placed right where the endorsement or link appears."

### Crypto-Specific Example
> "This post may contain some affiliate links, which means we may receive a commission if you purchase something that we recommend at no additional cost for you."

### Best Practices for Crypto Platforms
- Display conspicuously close to affiliate links
- Place on every page with affiliate content
- Always before affiliate links (so users know before clicking)
- Often paired with investment disclaimer for high-risk crypto niche

## 11. Risk Mitigation Strategies

### Highest Priority

1. **Never Provide Investment Advice**
   - No "buy" or "sell" recommendations
   - No portfolio allocation suggestions
   - No timing predictions ("now is the time to...")
   - All content labeled "for educational purposes only"

2. **Never Custody User Funds**
   - Do not hold cryptocurrency
   - Do not process payments for crypto purchases
   - Users trade on their own exchange accounts
   - PRUVIQ only provides data and tools

3. **Clear Service Boundaries**
   - State explicitly what PRUVIQ is NOT (advisor, broker, exchange)
   - Define service as "informational" and "educational"
   - Emphasize user responsibility for all decisions

4. **Comprehensive Disclaimers**
   - Visible on every page
   - Before high-risk content (strategies, backtests)
   - In API documentation
   - In email communications

### Medium Priority

5. **Affiliate Transparency**
   - Clear marking of all affiliate relationships
   - Disclosure before clicks, not just in footer
   - Statement that relationships don't affect data accuracy

6. **Data Accuracy Limitations**
   - Acknowledge possibility of errors
   - State data sources and third-party dependencies
   - Implement error reporting mechanism

7. **User Content Moderation** (if community features)
   - Clear rules against investment advice
   - Disclaimer that user content is not endorsed
   - Reporting mechanism for violations

### Lower Priority

8. **Jurisdiction-Specific Compliance**
   - Monitor changing regulations in key markets
   - Geo-block if necessary (certain US states, China, etc.)
   - Adapt disclaimers for local requirements

9. **Insurance**
   - Consider E&O (Errors & Omissions) insurance
   - Cyber liability insurance
   - General liability coverage

## 12. Common Legal Pitfalls to Avoid

### Critical Errors

❌ **"This coin will moon"** → ✅ **"Historical data shows [X], but past performance doesn't guarantee future results"**

❌ **"Buy now before it's too late"** → ✅ **"Users should conduct their own research and consider their risk tolerance"**

❌ **"Our strategy guarantees profits"** → ✅ **"Backtested results are hypothetical and may not reflect live trading"**

❌ **"We recommend [exchange]"** → ✅ **"[Exchange] is an available option [Affiliate]. Users should compare and choose based on their needs"**

❌ **Hidden affiliate relationships** → ✅ **Clear [Affiliate] badge on every link**

❌ **Collecting data without consent** → ✅ **Cookie consent before any non-essential tracking**

❌ **Unclear data usage** → ✅ **Specific disclosure of data purposes and recipients**

### Regulatory Triggers to Avoid

- Words like "guaranteed," "safe," "risk-free" in crypto context
- Specific price predictions or timing recommendations
- Personalized portfolio advice
- Holding or transferring user funds
- Unlicensed money transmission
- Operating as unregistered investment advisor
- Insider information or market manipulation
- False or misleading data
- Ignoring data breach notification requirements
